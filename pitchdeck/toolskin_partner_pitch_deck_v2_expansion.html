§04 · Workforce

Solo capacity. The hard math.

Sustainable solo budget: 45-55 hrs/week. After 12-16 weeks at this pace, burnout risk spikes hard. Plan accordingly.

Sales outreach + follow-up 10-12 hrs · 22%
Content production (writing, video, posts) 10-14 hrs · 26%
Service delivery (1 active project max) 15-25 hrs · 36%
Build / product improvements 5-8 hrs · 11%
Admin, support, comms 3-5 hrs · 5%
What gets cut
  • ×Multi-channel content (Twitter only)
  • ×Paid ads (no time to manage well)
  • ×Active community building (Discord, forums)
  • ×More than 2 active projects simultaneously
  • ×Conferences, podcasts, partnerships M1

§05 · Case studies

Mockups become credibility. Honestly.

You have 4 mockups (Higgsfield, YouTube Studio, Suno, userscript toolpanes) + Claude Design experiments. None are paid client work. All can be reframed as design system explorations — which is honest, defensible, and resonates with the Awwwards-tier audience more than fake client logos would.

Each case study page contains
  1. Hero — before/after screenshots
  2. The problem (existing UI characterization)
  3. The Toolskin solution (token map, how)
  4. Technical detail (hours, tokens used)
  5. Live demo — interactive token swap
  6. Disclaimer: design exploration, not paid work
The "founding customer" framing
"What I have is design system explorations on real-world UI surfaces. You'd be the first paid project — which means founding-customer pricing and direct access to me as the system's author."

Use for first 3-5 service contracts. Drop once you have real delivered work. Turns no-case-study into a feature.

The Claude Design experiments are bonus

"Here's what AI generates against Toolskin's token system. Notice how it stays on-brand even though no human styled these — because the tokens drive everything." → exact pitch for Vibecoder Designer segment. Use the dev-ide demo and ops dashboard as Exhibit A in cold outreach.

Disclaimer = strength · Honesty about exploration vs paid work resonates with picky-designer audience more than fake logos would

§06 · Marketing cadence

Weekly moves. Required. Not optional.

Twitter/X (primary channel)
  • +5 posts/week (token-swap demos, hot takes)
  • +2 threads/week (technical, philosophy)
  • +30 min/day engagement (no spam-pitches)
  • +Goal: 200 → 1,000 followers by month 3
LinkedIn (WP segment)
  • +2 posts/week (case-study format)
  • +10 connection requests/week
  • +Engage WP Slack groups daily
Content publishing
  • +1 blog post/week on toolskin.org/blog
  • +1 video/week (<90 sec, token-swap focus)
  • +Cross-post to dev.to, Hashnode
Launch moments (one-time)
  • W1 → Personal Twitter announcement
  • W2 → ProductHunt launch (Tue-Thu)
  • W3 → "Show HN" submission
  • W4 → First case study from delivery
Outreach quality, not quantity

Researched outreach (specific reference to recipient's work + non-pitchy framing) = 3-8% reply rate. Generic outreach = 0.5-1.5%. The difference is 5-10× revenue at the same volume. 50 quality DMs > 200 generic DMs.

Skip these and the funnel math doesn't deliver · They're inputs, not aspirations
