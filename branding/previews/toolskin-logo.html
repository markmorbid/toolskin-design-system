Toolskin · Logo · Proposals v2

Three harmonic directions

All concepts ship with the uploaded variable fonts (Space Grotesk + JetBrains Mono), use only the canonical orange #FF5500, and resolve cleanly from 380 px hero down to 16 px favicon. Pick one — it becomes the master mark.

01The Bracket

A wordmark wrapped in [ ] like a CSS attribute selector. The orange caret between TOOL and SKIN is the cursor — the active layer being typed into existence. Pure type, surgical geometry, zero decorative shapes.

Concept
A CSS attribute selector wrapped around the brand. [TOOL|SKIN] reads as a query: "the skin layer applied to the tool."
Why it harmonizes
The bracket weight matches the wordmark stroke. The caret matches the SKIN x-height. Everything sits on the same baseline grid.
Scaling
Reads at 16 px favicon as [ | ] — three vertical strokes with one orange. Survives down to a single browser tab.
02The Blade

A single 18° orange cut sliced clean through the wordmark — the brief's "Stanley blade, not Swiss watch" made literal. The blade angle (18°) equals the brand hue. One incision; everything else is type.

Concept
A precision tool cut through the wordmark. The blade is the edge of the system — what separates skin from substrate.
Why it harmonizes
Blade angle = 18°, the brand hue. Slice falls between L and S — the typographic seam between TOOL and SKIN. One angular gesture, no extra geometry.

At icon size the wordmark drops; the orange blade on dark square remains. Aggressive, unmistakable, instantly distinct in a tab strip.
03The Cascade

The layer-stack idea, harmonised. Three nested concentric rectangles — primitive, computed, component — locked together as one continuous structural form. The orange middle layer is the engine. Mark + wordmark share a single grid.

Concept
Three nested layers — primitive (frame), computed (orange engine), component (slot). One self-contained mark; the wordmark sits beside, never inside.
Why it harmonizes
Each layer is offset by exactly 18 px (= 16 px grid + 2 px stroke). All radii follow the radius scale. Bullet-proof on a 4-pt grid.
Scaling
At 16 px the inner slot drops; the ring + filled square remain — still legible as a layered stack. Highest density of meaning per pixel.
